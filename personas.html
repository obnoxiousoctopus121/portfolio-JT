the interface

Ten One Tea House, a boba shop on Thayer street, serves a number of customers everyday. The establishment has a unique self-ordering kiosk that provides customers with an additional ordering option. The kiosk is trying to solve issues regarding barriers to ordering. It allows users to order quickly, solving the problem of slow wait times.

understanding users

In order to design to user needs, it is important to understand how users interact with the order kiosk. I wanted to identify user goals, user intentions, and interface strengths and drawbacks. To examine the efficacy of the interface, I asked users the following questions and received the following answers:

1. Why did you decide to order at the kiosk instead of at the register?

🌱 Users did not want to socialize with workers and wanted to preserve their social battery
🌱 Users did not know what they wanted to order and were afraid of holding up the line
🌱 Users did not want to wait in line to order
🌱 Users wanted more autonomy and time to look through the menu and customization features


2. What did you expect would happen after ordering at the kiosk?

🌱 Users expected to receive their drink with little Interaction with workers
🌱 Users expected to wait around 5 to 10 minutes for their drinks


3. How did you expect the kiosk to work?

🌱 Users expected the touch screen to be responsive to taps
🌱 Users expected to scroll through the menu and tap which drink and customizations they desired


4. How did you know that you can order at the kiosk?

🌱 Users saw other people using the kiosk instead of ordering at the register
🌱 Users saw the screen sign say "Tap to Order"

developing personas and story boards

Based off these answers, I was able to create personas: Regular Ryan and Indecisive Iris. These two personas represent a potential self-ordering kiosk user. Understanding users like Ryan and Iris help us design towards user needs. In addition, it is valuable to understand their user journeys through storyboarding.

key takeaways

Exploring the user experience of the self-ordering kiosk at Ten One Tea helped me better understand the importance of human-centered design. When designing products or interfaces, the research process is an integral part of understanding how to best meet the needs of the intended audience. By employing methods like user interviews, obseriving user interactions, and developing user personas, designers can design better products. Key skills included user research, user interviews, and storyboarding.
